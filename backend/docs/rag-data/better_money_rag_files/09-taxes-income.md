## Preparing and Filing Taxes

**Source URL:** https://bettermoneyhabits.bankofamerica.com/en/taxes-income?subTopicId=prep-and-filing

### Tax Preparation Basics
- Gather all necessary documents: W-2s, 1099s, receipts for deductions, prior year returns
- Decide whether to file yourself (using tax software) or hire a professional
- Understand filing status options: single, married filing jointly, married filing separately, head of household, qualifying surviving spouse
- Know key deadlines — the federal tax filing deadline is typically April 15
- Consider whether to itemize deductions or take the standard deduction
- File electronically for faster processing and quicker refunds
- Keep copies of filed returns and supporting documents for at least three years

## Financial Decisions That Affect Your Taxes

**Source URL:** https://bettermoneyhabits.bankofamerica.com/en/taxes-income/events-that-can-affect-your-taxes
**Read Time:** 5 minutes

### Five Key Decisions with Tax Implications

1. **Getting married or divorced** — Filing status changes can affect tax brackets, deductions, and credits. Married couples should compare filing jointly vs. separately.
2. **Buying or selling a home** — Mortgage interest and property taxes may be deductible. Capital gains on home sales may be partially excluded from income.
3. **Having children** — Dependents may qualify for the Child Tax Credit and other benefits. Education expenses may offer additional deductions or credits.
4. **Changing jobs or starting a business** — Self-employment income has different tax obligations (estimated quarterly payments, self-employment tax). Job-related moving expenses may have implications.
5. **Making investment decisions** — Realized capital gains and losses affect taxable income. Tax-loss harvesting can offset gains. Retirement account contributions may reduce taxable income.

## New Job Checklist: Employee Benefits

**Source URL:** https://bettermoneyhabits.bankofamerica.com/en/taxes-income/new-job-checklist

### Making the Most of Your Benefits Package

- **Health insurance** — Compare plan options (HMO, PPO, HDHP) during the enrollment period. Consider premiums, deductibles, copays, and network coverage.
- **Retirement plans** — Enroll in the 401(k) or similar plan as soon as eligible. Contribute at least enough to capture the full employer match.
- **Health Savings Account (HSA)** — Available with high-deductible health plans. Offers triple tax advantage: tax-deductible contributions, tax-free growth, and tax-free withdrawals for qualified medical expenses.
- **Flexible Spending Account (FSA)** — Use pre-tax dollars for medical or dependent care expenses. Be aware of "use it or lose it" rules.
- **Life and disability insurance** — Evaluate employer-provided coverage and determine if supplemental coverage is needed.
- **Paid time off** — Understand vacation, sick leave, and personal day policies.
- **Education benefits** — Look for tuition reimbursement, student loan repayment assistance, or professional development funds.

## Understanding Your Paycheck

**Source URL:** https://bettermoneyhabits.bankofamerica.com/en/taxes-income/how-to-read-a-paycheck

### Common Paycheck Deductions
- **Federal income tax** — Withheld based on W-4 elections and income level
- **State and local income tax** — Varies by state and municipality (some states have no income tax)
- **Social Security tax (FICA)** — 6.2% of wages up to the annual wage base limit
- **Medicare tax** — 1.45% of all wages (additional 0.9% for high earners)
- **Pre-tax deductions** — 401(k) contributions, health insurance premiums, HSA/FSA contributions
- **Post-tax deductions** — Roth 401(k) contributions, life insurance, garnishments

### Tips
- Review your W-4 annually and after major life changes to ensure proper withholding
- Getting a large refund means too much is being withheld — adjust to keep more per paycheck
- Owing a large amount at filing means too little is withheld — increase withholding to avoid penalties

## Career and Financial Life

**Source URL:** https://bettermoneyhabits.bankofamerica.com/en/financial-tips-for-starting-your-career

### Financial Tips for Early Career
- Create a budget from the first paycheck
- Start an emergency fund immediately
- Begin retirement contributions as early as possible (even small amounts benefit from decades of compound growth)
- Pay attention to employer benefits and use them fully
- Manage student loan payments strategically
- Avoid lifestyle inflation — resist increasing spending proportionally with every raise
- Build and maintain a good credit history
