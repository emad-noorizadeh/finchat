## Understanding Credit Scores

**Source URL:** https://bettermoneyhabits.bankofamerica.com/en/credit/how-credit-score-is-calculated
**Read Time:** 4 minutes

### What Is a Credit Score?

A credit score is a numerical representation of creditworthiness, typically ranging from 300 to 850. Lenders use it to evaluate the risk of lending money. Higher scores generally lead to better loan terms and interest rates.

### Factors That Affect Credit Scores

Credit scores are calculated based on several key factors:
- **Payment history** — Whether bills and debts are paid on time (most significant factor)
- **Credit utilization** — The ratio of credit used to total credit available
- **Length of credit history** — How long accounts have been open
- **Credit mix** — Variety of credit types (credit cards, mortgages, auto loans, etc.)
- **New credit inquiries** — How many new accounts or applications have been made recently

## Improving Your Credit Score

**Source URL:** https://bettermoneyhabits.bankofamerica.com/en/credit/how-to-improve-your-credit-score
**Read Time:** 4 minutes

### Five Key Strategies

1. **Pay bills on time** — Payment history has the largest impact on credit scores. Set up autopay or reminders to avoid missed payments.
2. **Reduce credit utilization** — Aim to keep balances below 30% of available credit limits. Paying down existing balances helps.
3. **Don't close old accounts** — Length of credit history matters. Keeping older accounts open (even if unused) can help.
4. **Limit new credit applications** — Each hard inquiry can temporarily lower a score. Only apply for credit when needed.
5. **Monitor credit reports** — Check reports regularly for errors and dispute any inaccuracies.

## Building Credit from Scratch

**Source URL:** https://bettermoneyhabits.bankofamerica.com/en/credit/start-building-credit
**Read Time:** 4 minutes (video)

### Getting Started Without Credit History

Building credit for the first time requires establishing a track record. Common starting strategies include:
- Applying for a secured credit card (backed by a cash deposit)
- Becoming an authorized user on a family member's card
- Taking out a credit-builder loan
- Ensuring rent and utility payments are reported to credit bureaus where possible

## Choosing a Credit Card

**Source URL:** https://bettermoneyhabits.bankofamerica.com/en/credit/finding-the-right-credit-card

### Factors to Consider
- Annual fees vs. no annual fee options
- Interest rates (APR) — especially if carrying a balance
- Rewards programs (cash back, points, travel miles)
- Introductory offers (0% APR periods, sign-up bonuses)
- Credit limit offered
- Additional benefits (purchase protection, travel insurance, etc.)

## Tips for Your First Credit Card

**Source URL:** https://bettermoneyhabits.bankofamerica.com/en/credit/first-credit-card
**Read Time:** Article

### Seven Key Tips
1. Understand how interest and minimum payments work
2. Pay the full balance each month when possible
3. Keep utilization low relative to the credit limit
4. Set up payment reminders or autopay
5. Read the card's terms and conditions carefully
6. Track spending to avoid exceeding comfortable limits
7. Use the card responsibly to build a strong credit history

## Credit Card Cash Advances

**Source URL:** https://bettermoneyhabits.bankofamerica.com/en/credit/what-is-a-credit-card-cash-advance

### What to Know
- A cash advance allows borrowing cash against a credit card's credit line
- Cash advances typically carry higher interest rates than regular purchases
- Interest usually begins accruing immediately (no grace period)
- Additional fees often apply
- Generally considered a last resort due to high costs
