## Checking vs. Savings Accounts

**Source URL:** https://bettermoneyhabits.bankofamerica.com/en/personal-banking/the-difference-between-checking-and-savings-account
**Read Time:** 5 minutes

### Key Differences

- **Checking accounts** are designed for frequent transactions — paying bills, daily purchases, writing checks, and ATM withdrawals. They typically offer debit card access and may or may not earn interest.
- **Savings accounts** are designed for storing money and earning interest. They may have limits on certain types of withdrawals and are intended for funds not needed for daily spending.
- Many financial advisors recommend having both: a checking account for daily needs and a savings account for goals and emergencies.

## Common Bank Fees and How to Avoid Them

**Source URL:** https://bettermoneyhabits.bankofamerica.com/en/personal-banking/avoid-bank-fees
**Read Time:** 5 minutes

### Eight Common Fees

1. **Monthly maintenance fees** — Often waivable by maintaining a minimum balance, setting up direct deposit, or meeting other qualifying criteria.
2. **Overdraft fees** — Charged when spending exceeds the account balance. Opt out of overdraft coverage or link to a savings account for protection.
3. **ATM fees** — Using out-of-network ATMs incurs fees from both the ATM operator and your bank. Use in-network ATMs or get cash back at point of sale.
4. **Wire transfer fees** — Charged for sending or receiving wire transfers. Use ACH transfers or digital payment services for lower-cost alternatives.
5. **Foreign transaction fees** — Assessed on purchases made in foreign currencies. Some accounts and credit cards waive these fees.
6. **Paper statement fees** — Some banks charge for mailed statements. Enroll in electronic statements to avoid.
7. **Account closing fees** — Some banks charge if an account is closed within a certain period of opening. Read terms before opening.
8. **Returned deposit fees** — Charged when a deposited check bounces.

## Certificates of Deposit (CDs)

**Source URL:** https://bettermoneyhabits.bankofamerica.com/en/personal-banking/what-is-a-cd-investment

### How CDs Work
- A CD is a time-based deposit with a bank that pays a fixed interest rate
- Terms typically range from a few months to several years
- Generally offers higher interest rates than regular savings accounts
- Withdrawing funds before the term ends usually results in an early withdrawal penalty
- FDIC insured up to applicable limits
- Best suited for money you won't need during the CD's term

## Questions to Ask About Checking Accounts

**Source URL:** https://bettermoneyhabits.bankofamerica.com/en/personal-banking/choosing-bank-account-questions
**Read Time:** 4 minutes

### Ten Key Questions
1. What are the monthly fees, and how can they be waived?
2. What is the minimum balance requirement?
3. Is there a robust ATM network?
4. What mobile and online banking features are available?
5. Are there overdraft protection options?
6. What are the interest rates (if any)?
7. How are deposits handled (mobile deposit, direct deposit)?
8. What customer service options exist (branch, phone, chat)?
9. Are there any introductory offers or rewards?
10. What additional services are included (bill pay, person-to-person transfers)?

## Managing Bank Accounts

**Source URL:** https://bettermoneyhabits.bankofamerica.com/en/personal-banking?subTopicId=managing-bank-accounts

### Best Practices
- Monitor accounts regularly through online or mobile banking
- Set up alerts for low balances, large transactions, and deposits
- Reconcile accounts monthly
- Keep checking and savings accounts properly funded
- Review and update beneficiary information periodically
