## Online and Mobile Security Tips

**Source URL:** https://bettermoneyhabits.bankofamerica.com/en/privacy-security/online-security-privacy-tips

### Ten Security Best Practices

1. **Use strong, unique passwords** — Combine uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols. Use a different password for each account.
2. **Enable multi-factor authentication (MFA)** — Add a second layer of verification beyond just a password.
3. **Keep software updated** — Install operating system, browser, and app updates promptly to patch security vulnerabilities.
4. **Be cautious with public Wi-Fi** — Avoid accessing financial accounts on unsecured networks. Use a VPN if necessary.
5. **Monitor accounts regularly** — Check bank and credit card statements frequently for unauthorized transactions.
6. **Set up account alerts** — Enable notifications for transactions, login attempts, and balance changes.
7. **Beware of phishing** — Don't click links or download attachments from unexpected emails or texts. Verify the sender independently.
8. **Secure mobile devices** — Use screen locks, enable remote wipe capabilities, and only download apps from official stores.
9. **Shred sensitive documents** — Dispose of paper documents containing personal or financial information securely.
10. **Review privacy settings** — Regularly check privacy settings on social media and online accounts.

## Scam Awareness

**Source URL:** https://bettermoneyhabits.bankofamerica.com/en/privacy-security/latest-scams-to-watch-out-for
**Read Time:** 4 minutes

### Common Scam Types to Watch For
- **Phishing emails and texts** — Messages impersonating banks, government agencies, or companies to steal login credentials or personal information
- **Social engineering** — Manipulative tactics that exploit trust or urgency to trick people into revealing information or sending money
- **Romance scams** — Fake online relationships designed to extract money
- **Tech support scams** — Callers or pop-ups claiming your computer has a virus and requesting remote access or payment
- **Investment scams** — Promises of guaranteed high returns with no risk

### Red Flags
- Unexpected urgency or pressure to act immediately
- Requests for payment via gift cards, wire transfers, or cryptocurrency
- Unsolicited requests for personal information (Social Security numbers, passwords, PINs)
- Communications with poor grammar or suspicious email addresses
- Offers that seem too good to be true

## Identity Theft Protection

**Source URL:** https://bettermoneyhabits.bankofamerica.com/en/privacy-security?subTopicId=identity-theft

### Prevention Strategies
- Freeze credit with all three major bureaus (Equifax, Experian, TransUnion) when not actively applying for credit
- Monitor credit reports regularly (free annual reports available at AnnualCreditReport.com)
- Protect Social Security number — only share when absolutely necessary
- Use secure mail or electronic delivery for financial statements
- Be careful about what personal information is shared online and on social media

### If You're a Victim
- Contact financial institutions immediately to freeze or close compromised accounts
- File a report with the Federal Trade Commission (FTC) at IdentityTheft.gov
- Place a fraud alert on credit reports
- File a police report for documentation
- Keep detailed records of all communications and steps taken

## Protecting Privacy and Security

**Source URL:** https://bettermoneyhabits.bankofamerica.com/en/privacy-security?subTopicId=protecting-privacy-security

### Digital Privacy Practices
- Review app permissions and limit access to location, contacts, and camera when not needed
- Use privacy-focused browsers or browser extensions
- Be selective about sharing personal information with online services
- Read privacy policies for services that handle sensitive data
- Consider using a password manager for secure credential storage
