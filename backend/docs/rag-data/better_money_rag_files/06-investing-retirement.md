## 401(k) Plans: Key Things to Know

**Source URL:** https://bettermoneyhabits.bankofamerica.com/en/retirement/401k-benefits-and-information
**Read Time:** 3 minutes

### Ten Questions to Ask About Your 401(k)

1. Does the employer offer matching contributions? If so, what is the match formula?
2. What is the vesting schedule for employer contributions?
3. What investment options are available within the plan?
4. What are the plan's fees and expense ratios?
5. Is a Roth 401(k) option available?
6. What is the maximum contribution limit for the current year?
7. Can catch-up contributions be made (for those 50+)?
8. What are the rules for loans or hardship withdrawals?
9. How are distributions handled in retirement?
10. What happens to the 401(k) when leaving the employer?

### Key Benefits
- Tax-advantaged growth (traditional pre-tax or Roth after-tax contributions)
- Employer matching is essentially free money — contribute enough to get the full match
- Automatic payroll deductions make saving consistent
- Higher contribution limits than IRAs

## Individual Retirement Accounts (IRAs)

**Source URL:** https://bettermoneyhabits.bankofamerica.com/en/retirement/what-is-an-ira

### Traditional IRA vs. Roth IRA

| Feature | Traditional IRA | Roth IRA |
|---------|----------------|----------|
| Contributions | Pre-tax (may be tax-deductible) | After-tax (not deductible) |
| Growth | Tax-deferred | Tax-free |
| Withdrawals in retirement | Taxed as ordinary income | Tax-free (if qualified) |
| Required Minimum Distributions | Yes (starting at age 73) | None during owner's lifetime |
| Income limits for contributions | None (deductibility may be limited) | Yes — high earners may be phased out |

### Key Considerations
- IRAs provide additional tax-advantaged retirement savings beyond employer plans
- Contribution limits are lower than 401(k) plans
- Early withdrawals (before 59½) generally incur a 10% penalty plus taxes
- Some exceptions exist for first-time home purchases, education expenses, etc.

## Asset Allocation

**Source URL:** https://bettermoneyhabits.bankofamerica.com/en/retirement/asset-allocation

### Choosing the Right Investment Mix

Asset allocation is the strategy of dividing investments among different asset classes to balance risk and reward. Key principles include:

- **Diversification** — Spreading investments across stocks, bonds, and cash equivalents reduces the impact of any single investment's poor performance
- **Risk tolerance** — Younger investors with longer time horizons can generally afford more risk (higher stock allocation); those nearing retirement typically shift toward more conservative investments
- **Rebalancing** — Periodically adjusting the portfolio back to target allocations as market movements shift the mix
- **Time horizon** — The number of years until you need the money is a primary factor in determining appropriate allocation

## Building Wealth

**Source URL:** https://bettermoneyhabits.bankofamerica.com/en/retirement?subTopicId=building-wealth

### Core Wealth-Building Principles
- Start investing as early as possible to maximize compound growth
- Contribute consistently, even in small amounts
- Take advantage of tax-advantaged accounts (401(k), IRA, HSA)
- Keep investment fees low
- Maintain a long-term perspective and avoid emotional reactions to market volatility
- Reinvest dividends and capital gains

## Nearing Retirement

**Source URL:** https://bettermoneyhabits.bankofamerica.com/en/retirement?subTopicId=nearing-retirement

### Planning Steps for Those Close to Retirement
- Estimate retirement income needs (a common guideline: 70–80% of pre-retirement income)
- Review Social Security benefits and optimal claiming strategy
- Assess healthcare coverage and Medicare options
- Create a retirement income plan drawing from multiple sources
- Consider gradually shifting investments to more conservative allocations
- Plan for Required Minimum Distributions from tax-deferred accounts
