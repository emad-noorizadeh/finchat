## Marriage and Money

**Source URL:** https://bettermoneyhabits.bankofamerica.com/en/saving-budgeting/how-to-manage-money-in-marriage

### Ten Tips for Financial Harmony
1. Have open, honest conversations about money before and during marriage
2. Discuss and align on financial goals together
3. Decide on a system: joint accounts, separate accounts, or a combination
4. Create a household budget together
5. Determine how to divide financial responsibilities
6. Maintain individual financial identity (separate credit history)
7. Plan for major expenses as a team
8. Set spending limits that require mutual discussion
9. Schedule regular financial check-ins
10. Plan for the unexpected with insurance and emergency savings

## Joint Bank Accounts

**Source URL:** https://bettermoneyhabits.bankofamerica.com/en/saving-budgeting/joint-bank-account

### What to Know
- Both account holders have full access to deposits and withdrawals
- Both are equally responsible for the account
- Can simplify shared household expenses and bill payment
- Consider maintaining some individual accounts for personal spending
- Both account holders' credit may be affected by the account's status
- Discuss expectations and ground rules before opening a joint account

## Paycheck-to-Paycheck Living

**Source URL:** https://bettermoneyhabits.bankofamerica.com/en/how-to-stop-living-paycheck-to-paycheck
**Read Time:** 9 minutes, 11 resources

### Breaking the Cycle
- Track all spending to identify where money goes
- Reduce non-essential expenses, even temporarily
- Build a small emergency buffer (start with $500–$1,000 as an initial goal)
- Look for ways to increase income (side jobs, negotiating raises, selling unused items)
- Automate even tiny savings contributions
- Address high-interest debt that drains monthly cash flow
- Use free financial education resources and counseling services

## Financial Emergency Planning

**Source URL:** https://bettermoneyhabits.bankofamerica.com/en/dealing-with-a-financial-emergency

### Steps to Take During a Financial Emergency
- Assess the situation calmly and determine the immediate financial need
- Review available resources: emergency fund, insurance coverage, credit options
- Contact creditors proactively to discuss hardship options or payment plans
- Explore community resources: non-profit financial counseling, government assistance programs
- Prioritize essential expenses: housing, food, utilities, medical needs
- Create a recovery plan once the immediate emergency is addressed
- Use the experience to strengthen future emergency preparedness
