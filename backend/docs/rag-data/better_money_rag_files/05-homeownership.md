## Buying a Home Within Your Comfort Zone

**Source URL:** https://bettermoneyhabits.bankofamerica.com/en/home-ownership/buying-home-comfortably-affordably

### Key Considerations
- Determine how much house you can truly afford beyond just what a lender approves
- Factor in all costs: mortgage payment, property taxes, insurance, maintenance, HOA fees
- Maintain an emergency fund even after the down payment
- Consider the impact on other financial goals (retirement, education savings)
- Account for potential changes in income or expenses

## What to Consider When Buying Your First Home

**Source URL:** https://bettermoneyhabits.bankofamerica.com/en/what-to-consider-when-buying-your-first-home
**Read Time:** 1 minute overview, 10 resources

### First-Time Buyer Checklist
- Check and improve credit score before applying for a mortgage
- Save for a down payment (explore programs for first-time buyers)
- Get pre-approved for a mortgage to understand budget
- Research neighborhoods and property types
- Understand the full homebuying process from offer to closing
- Budget for closing costs (typically 2–5% of the purchase price)
- Plan for ongoing maintenance and repair costs

## Real Estate Terminology

**Source URL:** https://bettermoneyhabits.bankofamerica.com/en/home-ownership/5-stage-guide-to-homebuying-terms

### Essential Terms to Know
- **Appraisal** — Professional assessment of a property's market value
- **Closing costs** — Fees and expenses paid at the completion of a real estate transaction
- **Down payment** — The upfront portion of the purchase price paid by the buyer
- **Earnest money** — A deposit demonstrating the buyer's serious intent to purchase
- **Escrow** — A third-party account holding funds during the transaction process
- **Fixed-rate mortgage** — A loan with an interest rate that remains constant for the entire term
- **Adjustable-rate mortgage (ARM)** — A loan whose interest rate can change after an initial fixed period
- **Pre-approval** — A lender's conditional commitment to lend a specific amount
- **Title insurance** — Protection against claims on the property's legal ownership
- **PMI (Private Mortgage Insurance)** — Required insurance when the down payment is less than 20%

## Closing Costs

**Source URL:** https://bettermoneyhabits.bankofamerica.com/en/home-ownership/closer-look-at-closing-costs

### What Closing Costs Include
- Loan origination fees
- Appraisal and inspection fees
- Title search and title insurance
- Attorney fees (where required)
- Recording fees
- Prepaid taxes and insurance
- Mortgage insurance premiums (if applicable)

### Tips for Managing Closing Costs
- Request a Loan Estimate from the lender early in the process
- Compare closing cost estimates from multiple lenders
- Negotiate with the seller to cover part of closing costs
- Ask about lender credits in exchange for a slightly higher interest rate

## Home Equity Lines of Credit (HELOC)

**Source URL:** https://bettermoneyhabits.bankofamerica.com/en/home-ownership/benefits-of-using-home-equity
**Read Time:** 3 minutes

### Benefits and Risks
- A HELOC allows homeowners to borrow against the equity in their home
- Typically offers lower interest rates than credit cards or personal loans
- Interest may be tax-deductible in certain circumstances (consult a tax advisor)
- Flexible borrowing: draw funds as needed during the draw period
- **Risk:** Your home serves as collateral — failure to repay could result in foreclosure
- Variable interest rates mean payments can increase over time
