## Buying a Car

**Source URL:** https://bettermoneyhabits.bankofamerica.com/en/auto?subTopicId=buying-a-car

### Key Steps in the Car Buying Process

1. **Determine your budget** — Consider the total cost of ownership, not just the monthly payment. Include insurance, fuel, maintenance, and registration.
2. **Check your credit** — Your credit score significantly affects the interest rate offered on an auto loan. Improve it before applying if possible.
3. **Get pre-approved for financing** — Shop for loan rates at banks and credit unions before visiting dealerships. This gives negotiating leverage.
4. **Research vehicles** — Compare reliability ratings, fuel efficiency, safety features, and resale value.
5. **New vs. used** — New cars offer warranties and the latest features but depreciate faster. Used cars offer better value but may need more maintenance.
6. **Negotiate the price** — Focus on the total purchase price rather than the monthly payment. Research fair market value before negotiating.
7. **Review the contract carefully** — Understand all fees, add-ons, and financing terms before signing.

## Electric Vehicle Costs

**Source URL:** https://bettermoneyhabits.bankofamerica.com/en/auto/real-cost-of-buying-an-electric-car

### Total Cost of Ownership Considerations
- **Purchase price** — EVs often have higher sticker prices, but federal and state tax credits may offset the difference
- **Fuel savings** — Electricity costs significantly less per mile than gasoline
- **Maintenance** — EVs generally require less maintenance (no oil changes, fewer brake replacements due to regenerative braking)
- **Insurance** — May be slightly higher due to higher vehicle value and specialized repair costs
- **Charging infrastructure** — Home charging installation costs; public charging network availability in your area
- **Battery life and replacement** — Battery degradation over time; replacement costs if needed outside warranty
- **Resale value** — Varies by brand and model; the EV market is evolving rapidly
